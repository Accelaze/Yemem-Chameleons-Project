Satisfied But Alert

Chameleons turn this colour when they are comfortable with their environment but are also alert incase of any imminent or sudden danger.

They have excellent grip on the branches they are perched on and will easily hide withing the plants and foliage.

They're eyes are constantly scanning the place for any danger, healthy chameleons NEVER have their eyes closed during the day.

Its tail serves two purposes; they can tightly grip onto branches and serve as aid for balance when walking/climbing on branches.

Different chameleons have different patterns when showing stress/anger. They can show dramatic spots or go black/dark.

Fear Induced

When a chameleon is scared or feeling threatened there are several things it will do purely out of survival intinct.

It will flatten its body to look bigger to any predator (or you if it does not feel safe with you) and puff out its Gular (throat) to look bigger.

It will gape its jaws (open the mouth and clearly show teeth) to show it means business.

It'll put one of its front legs up in a protective position. If threat increases it'll put both front legs up.

It'll turn into bright colours ( orblack spots if stressed or grabbed) to warn the intruder away.

If you continue if whatever you are doing or go closer to the chameleon it'll hiss at you.

If you these signs are ignored the chameleon will either bite you or show the traits of a sick chameleon.
